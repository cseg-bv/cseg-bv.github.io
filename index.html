Welcome!
We're a local homeschool family with young children (Kindergarten-6th grade) enrolled in the Classical Conversations (CC) Foundations & Essentials (F/E) program. Instead of continuing with the CC Challenge program we're creating a separate, independent homeschool program for 7-12th graders with the following principles:

  • Christian based
  • Synchronized with CC F/E Program
    • Our goal is to support parents in our homeschool community who wish to keep their K-6th grade students in the CC F/E program, but who wish to enroll their 7-12th grade students in our STEM and Humanities & Social Sciences programs. We will offer weekly classes on the same day and in the same facility as the CC F/E program (but without interfering with the CC F/E program).
  • Comprehensive 7-12th Grade Curriculum
    • Morning Humanities & Social Sciences (HSS) program covering logic, world history & literature, language arts, public speaking, etc.
    • Afternoon STEM program covering core sciences, technology & engineering, mathematics, etc.
    • We are currently offering 7-9th grade programs, and beginning Fall 2027 we will also offer 10-12th grade programs.
  • Enrollment & Attendance Flexibility
    • Students can enroll in only the STEM program, only the HSS program, or both.
    • Enrolled students can take an a la carte approach to attending classes in their program. For example, a student enrolled in the STEM program could attend only the core sciences & STEM elective classes, but not the mathematics class.
    • Attendance is not required, but students are expected to keep up with the curriculum schedule.
  • Low Cost
    • We are operating as a not-for-profit organization to serve our local homeschool community. Program fees will only be collected to cover liability insurance, facility tithing & tutor salaries.
    • Required curriculum & lab materials are purchased separately by the student.
  • Extra Curricular Activities
    • We would like to provide an information resource for extra curricular activities in our community such as family field trips, science fairs, workshops offered by skilled parents on welding, woodworking, etc.
